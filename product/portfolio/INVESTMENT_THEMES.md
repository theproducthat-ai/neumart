# Investment Themes

**Version**: 2.0  
**Owner**: Product Lead

---

## Purpose

Investment themes define the strategic buckets into which the team allocates capacity. Themes ensure the team isn't only building features for today's loudest stakeholder — they maintain balance across growth, retention, operations, and platform health.

---

## Current Investment Themes

### Theme 1: Customer Growth & Acquisition
**Goal**: Make it easier to onboard new customers and drive first purchase

Features in this theme:
- Onboarding improvements
- Discovery and browsing enhancements
- Promotional tools
- Search and personalisation

**Target allocation**: 30% of capacity

---

### Theme 2: Customer Retention & Loyalty
**Goal**: Increase repeat purchase rate and customer lifetime value

Features in this theme:
- Loyalty/rewards programme
- Saved carts and lists
- Reorder functionality
- Personalised recommendations

**Target allocation**: 20% of capacity

---

### Theme 3: Operational Excellence
**Goal**: Make internal operations more efficient and reduce errors

Features in this theme:
- Admin console improvements
- Inventory management
- Order management workflow
- Delivery management

**Target allocation**: 20% of capacity

---

### Theme 4: Platform Health & Reliability
**Goal**: Maintain technical quality, performance, and security

Features in this theme:
- Tech debt
- Performance improvements
- Security hardening
- Monitoring and observability

**Target allocation**: 15% of capacity

---

### Theme 5: Commercial Enablement
**Goal**: Deliver client commitments and sales-enablement features

Features in this theme:
- Client-specific features
- Integration capabilities
- Reporting and analytics

**Target allocation**: 15% of capacity

## How to Use Themes in Prioritisation

When evaluating a request for the roadmap:
1. Identify which theme it belongs to
2. Check if that theme is over or under its target allocation
3. Use this as a tiebreaker when two features have similar priority scores

**Do not let any theme fall below 5% or exceed 50%** — that indicates the team is not balanced.
